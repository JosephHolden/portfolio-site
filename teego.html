TeeGo Designs

TeeGo is a clothing store, native app, designed for iOS. Whilst this project was certainly a test of all the skills I have learned as a UI Designer, it was a particularly good exercise in my Brand Design skills. The brief required me to design an online store app which users could use quickly and easily, perhaps whilst they’re on-the- go. So the design had be clean, simple and familiar. Furthermore, the branding had to friendly, inviting, reflect the content of the store and reinforce the usability of the app.

My Process

Research

As we’d already been provided our user stories as part of our UX deliverables, I began by doing some competitor research. I analysed other store apps, noted their key functions, anything we could incorporate into our designs, and anything we might want to avoid. I then also began to consider our brand guide.

Branding

Firstly I took note of the content of the store; unique, designer t-shirts. In order to reinforce the simplicity of the app I chose a simple colour palette, pleasant to look at and not at all distracting for the user. I then chose for a sans-serif typeface (helvetica neue) as it nice and clear, informal but not too informal. I set out a guide for use of imagery, as well our copy language and voice, and also guides on how to use our logo. All these guidelines were set out clearly in a document, which could therefore be accessed by the whole design team. This is extremely important in order to maintain consistency across the whole product.

User Flow

Using our user-stories I began to put together a user-flow diagram. This helps us understand how the app is going to work, the journey the user is going on and what screens we will be designing.

Low Fidelity Wireframes

Based on familiar and instinctive patters, I put together some wireframes, beginning with low-fidelity sketches, up to high-fidelity screens designed on Sketch. I included 3 key functions; user can login as a guest user, view sale items and view advanced filtering options. In order to be sure that these functions had been designed properly, I carried out a user-test with several different users from different backgrounds.

High Fidelity Wireframes

Using the results I then implemented some changes to the screens and began to construct my final mockups. Furthermore, I designed 2 different homescreens for the desktop screen, and carried out an A/B preference test. Fortunately, there was a clear favourite!

Final Screens

Satisfied that the correct changes were implemented, I then went on to create some mockup designs, using our final screens. This part of the process can be extremely enjoyable, but is also important in order to see your designs in context.

Conclusion

A truly enjoyable project, and I think it represents some of my best work in regards to designing simple solutions for the user and effective visual design. It’s also important to be aware of the necessity to have consistency across in design across a product. This product highlighted the real importance of a clear brand guide. Most of all, I hope that the design has really addressed the goals of our users, by providing clear and simple solutions.
